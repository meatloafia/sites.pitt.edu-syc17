Integrating Writing and Design Portfolio

Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

Our first assignment was to create a website utilizing html code and present it to the class.

Logo A/B/C

I created three logos to represent myself. I designed logos for a bakery if I were to open one. I used Canva for logo A and AdobeExpress for logos B and C.

UX Testing Logos

I interviewed three people about logos A/B/C, gathered info about users, and improved logos based on user feedback.

Cartoon Portrait

Using Adobe Illustrator, I recreated a photo of myself.

Good Design

Using Adobe InDesign, I annotated a poster that I believe was designed well and explained why each element is following a principle of good design.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Considering technology in the act of making is an important foundational concept for this course. Through utilizing design software such as Illustrator (cartoon yourself), InDesign (good design), AdobeExpress & Canva (logos), and Visual Studio Code (design beginnings) for these projects, I was able to further collect the skills needed to create digital designs.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

By learning the concepts of good design, I was able to utilize key principles while making the projects. Specifically, for the good design project, I noted principles such as contrast, use of negative space, repetition, and text alignment. When creating my logos, I mostly utilized negative space by minimalizing clutter and repetition by using the same color palette across the designs.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Workshopping is an important step in the making process. I felt I was able to engage with others in a workshop environment during class when we would give feedback on each others' designs. I was able to give and receive praises and criticisms which were very useful for revision.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision is another vital step in the making process. The project I practiced the most revision with was the cartoon portrait. The first draft I made was not very clean and lacked contrast and depth. After getting feedback on this, I was able to go back and tweak this piece.

5. Learn to present your work.

Sometimes it can be very difficult to find to right words to convey my creative process and provide evidence for design decisions. However, I felt I was able to present my work effectively in the ux testing logos project. By conducting A/B testing, I was able to improve my communication skills in the scope of presenting designs to others.

Short Form


Two-page Spread

Using a combination of InDesign and Photoshop, I was able to closely recreate a two page magazine spread.

Graphic Design Resume

We were assigned to create a resume that includes aspects of creative design, breaking out of the traditional resume style. For mine, I wanted to illustrate my interest in the intersection of design and medicine.

Advertisement

Using Photoshop, I created an advertisement for Yakult, a probiotic yogurt.

Client Project

The client project was the most labor intensive piece of this section due to the fact that we were assigned to meet with a real person outside of our classroom who wanted a design for their business's use. I was able to experience a little bit of the process that graphic designers go through when creating designs for a client (design proposals, A/B drafts, revising, representing).

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

In making the pieces for the short form portfolio, the principle I thought most about was ethics. When drawing inspiration from other people's work, it's important to remain ethical as to not steal anyone's creative property. I think oftentimes the line between plagiarism and imitation can be blurry. This was especially the case in the two page spread project. Through the process of creating the spread, I had to be intentional about the aspects I was copying (and giving credit to) vs just taking inspiration from.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Building off the base principles of good design, I was able to further apply these techniques to the short form portfolio pieces. A principle we talked about a lot was accessibility, which I kept in mind while making my graphic design resume. While adding a variety of colors and patterns can make a design more interesting, it's easy to forget that these additions can make designs inaccessible to certain groups of people. With my resume, I tried to keep a good balance of visual interest and accessibility. p>


3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Again, workshopping was an important step in my process of making. Without engaging with others in a workshop environment, I would not be able to get honest feedback about how to improve my designs.


4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision played a big part in every project, but especially in the advertisement project. My first draft of the ad was not very good or eye catching and after receiving feedback on this, I added pops of red and more visually interesting elements throughout the poster that made it function better as an advertisement.

5. Learn to present your work.

The client project was a substantial way that I was able to further learn how to present my work. Throughout the project, working and talking with my client and presenting my work to them was vital in making sure I was representing the client's business in the way they desired. Through presenting my first drafts to revising and presenting second and third drafts, I was able to explain my design decisions.

Long Form


Four-page Spread

Using InDesign, I created a mock up four page spread for an article about capsule wardobes.

Ten-Page Spread

Using a combination of mostly InDesign and Photoshop, I created a mock up twelve page spread for musical artist Lorde.

UX Design Branding Project

After conceptualizing a brand, I created A/B samples of various elements of my brand identity such as logos, slogans, color palettes, font families, and mission statements. After creating these samples, I did UX interviews to see which samples were more appealing to users.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

As this section of my portfolio was the most labor intensive, I was able to consider the role of rhetoric in making more than I have in past projects. For the magazine spreads, it was very important to think about effective design and what the goal of each spread was for the reader.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

I was able to leverage design principles cumulatively in making the ten page spread by thinking about everything we learned during the semester. When making these spreads, I focused on utilization of white space, readability and accessibility, contrast, and color. It was extremely important to utilize design principles for the long form portfolio because creating cohesive magazine spreads spanning multiples pages is very different from creating some of the shorter projects from the class.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

I was able to engage with others in a workshop environment the most during for the 10 page spread. I think I found it very daunting to have 5 full blank pages to design from scratch so after drafting designs, I made sure to talk to my classmates and friends about what they liked and didn't like about the spreads. Engaging in workshop environments is crucial for good magazine design because you are designing for the purpose of delivering information to an audience. More than just personally thinking I made a good design, I have to make sure a reader would think the same.


4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision was a big part of all projects in the long form portfolio. Every project started with a subpar draft that was slowly reworked and revised to get to a final product.

5. Learn to present your work.

I was able to learn the most about presenting my work through the UX design branding project. After creating the A/B samples and interviewing people, I put together a brand design study presentation to present to my classmates. I was able to go step by step through my process and explain the design decisions I made and how to effectively present information to an audience.
